Internships

Strategic Business Process Outsourcing Intern

Stanford Seed c/o Africa Tech Space | Nakuru, Kenya | July 2025 - September 2025

Africa Tech Space, or ATS, is a venture associated with the Rift Valley Institute of Business Studies, a technical school located in Nakuru, Kenya that trains students in a broad range of different skills required for jobs in Kenya. Africa Tech Space, specifically, focuses on providing students with technical training and real-world experience in the domains of technology and business, and one of Kenya's key industries in this field is Business Process Outsourcing (BPO). While the school was effecitvely training students to do the kinds of work required for BPO firms, students were having a hard time getting connected and hired to these positions.

As a Strategic Business Process Outsourcing Intern, I worked in a key role to spearhead the Strategic BPO Partnerships initiative, aiming to create a pathway by which graduates of the school trained in BPO-relevant skills could get connected to and hired by BPO firms. I worked closely with the ATS's leadership team to understand the needs of the school and its students, and then I conducted research on the BPO industry in Kenya to understand the needs of BPO firms and how the school's students could meet those needs. I then used this research to identify possible pathways to create BPO opportunities for the school, along with potential BPO partners for the school, and I reached out to these firms to establish partnerships that would allow the school's students to get connected to and hired by these firms. I also worked to create a framework and structure by which the school could create a consistent pathway for students to get work in BPO firms, and I worked with the school's leadership team to implement this framework and structure.

Product Design Intern

Hotspot, Inc. | Remote | June 2024 - August 2024

Hotspot was a social media startup focused on using AI to create engaging and personalized event suggestions for users to engage in with friends and connections. In the pre-seed stage, the startup was focused on building out their app for release on college campuses later that year. As an intern, I was the only hire specifically focused on product design for the app, tasked with creating a user-friendly interface and experience for the app.

As part of my responsibilities, I executed market research on the social media design landscape, with specific focus on the onboarding and event-planning flows. I also conducted user interviews and usability testing to inform both design and development decisions. Using the research and interview feedback, I then created wireframes and prototypes for key features and flows for the app, using both low-fidelity paper prototypes and high-fidelity digital Figma mockups. Throughout this process, as the de-facto product design lead for the app, I participated in key discussions with the development team regarding paroduct narrative, roadmap, and consumer experience to ensure a seamless integration of design and functionality.

Work Experience

Associate & Internal Manager

Stanford Student Enterprises | Stanford, CA | October 2021 - June 2024

Stanford Student Enterprises, or SSE, is a student-run organization that manages a variety of different operations on Stanford's campus, including student club operations, retail stores, and cafes. As an Associate and then an Internal Manager at SSE, I worked in a key role to manage the operations of one of the organization's most impactful ventures, Capital Group. Capital Group is the branch of SSE that oversees the finances of student clubs on campus, managing over $13 million in assets and providing financial services to over 700 student groups at Stanford. These services include reimbursements for club expenses, money transfers to vendors, and financial advising for club budgets.

Starting out as an Associate, my role involved processing initial transaction requests for student clubs, including for reimbursements and money transfers to pay vendors. In March 2022, I was promoted to Internal Manager, a leadership role in which I managed a team of 10 Associates and oversaw the internal operations of Capital Group. As part of this role, I was responsible for managing the workflow and processes of the team through trainings, check-ins, and teamwide events to ensure that we were able to efficiently and effectively process transactions for student clubs I also worked closely with the leadership team of Capital Group to ensure that our operations were aligned with the broader goals and values of Capital Group and SSE. Moreover, I conducted multiple office hour sessions for student club leaders to provide financial advising and support for their club budgets. Overall, my experience at SSE allowed me to develop strong leadership and management skills while contributing to the success of one of Stanford's most impactful student-run businesses.
